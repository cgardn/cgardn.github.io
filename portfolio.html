ONE/SIZE

Although I work on many brand sites and various other projects, the ONE/SIZE website is where I've invested most of my effort. I didn't do the initial build, but over the last 2 years I've brought the site from over 1.5 MB of JS bundle down to less than 200kB compressed and minified. At this point, most of what you see has been either refactored or completely rebuilt by me.

Most of the gains have been rewriting UI components with vanilla JS to eliminate wasteful usage of third-party libraries; one notable example was my predecessor including the entire GSAP library into the site bundle, but only using it for a slide carousel, which is a trivial task with CSS alone, or a little JS if you want scroll buttons.

Lately I've been wading into refactoring and splitting the CSS, as all of it is currently built into one gigantic file.

I've also led internal efforts to add file size limits to our content policy, in an attempt to keep above-the-fold images and gifs/videos to reasonable sizes for our mobile users.
